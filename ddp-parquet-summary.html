DDP Daily Population Data — Parquet Summary & Integration

Published

April 11, 2026

Overview

This document summarizes the Deportation Data Project (DDP) daily detention population dataset in parquet format (data/ddp/facilities-daily-population-latest.parquet). The parquet file is the current data source for FY26 and FY24 comparison analyses against ICE’s published annual detention statistics.

The dataset spans October 2022 through March 2026. The first and last dates on which any facility recorded nonzero detention are 2022-10-01 and 2026-03-10.

Mean daily population is the aggregate across all facility codes. The median facility-day observation is zero, reflecting the large number of hold rooms, staging areas, and low-utilization codes in the data. FY2026 covers October 1, 2025 through March 10, 2026 only.

The feather-only codes are almost entirely medical facilities (227 of 233). The parquet-only codes are predominantly non-dedicated jails (36), federal facilities (13), and hold/staging codes (6); 28 remain unclassified, meaning they have no match in any of the project’s type reference files.

Feather vs. Parquet Comparison

The feather file covers September 1, 2023 – October 15, 2025. The parquet file covers October 1, 2022 – March 10, 2026. Their overlap period is September 1, 2023 – October 15, 2025 (776 days).

Aggregate daily population

Over the overlap period, the parquet total is on average 209 detainees per day lower than the feather total (feather mean: 44,607; parquet mean: 44,398). No two days are exactly equal. The largest single-day absolute difference is 1,773. The difference is consistent with the feather-only codes contributing approximately 60 ADP during this period — almost all from medical facilities. The parquet-only codes contributed approximately 1 ADP in total over the same period.

Facility-level agreement

For the 620 codes present in both datasets, over the overlap period:

Agreement band Facilities
Exactly equal 410
Within 1% 562
Within 5% 601
Discrepant ≥ 5% 19

Mean absolute difference for shared codes: 0.28 ADP.

The 19 discrepant facilities include several with very low ADP (< 2) where small absolute differences produce large percentages, and one substantive case: Florida Soft-Sided Facility (FLDSSFS), which shows 106 ADP in the feather vs. 26 ADP in the parquet over the overlap period.

Data Integration Summary

Pipeline Targets

The feather file remains the source for ddp_raw and all existing FY25-era pipeline targets. The parquet file is loaded as a separate ddp_new target for FY26 (and future FY24) comparisons:

tar_target(ddp_parquet_file,
  here::here("data/ddp/facilities-daily-population-latest.parquet"),
  format = "file")

tar_target(ddp_new, arrow::read_parquet(ddp_parquet_file))

The ddp_facility_summary() function handles both sources; the state column it previously required is treated as optional.

Canonical ID Matching

DDP facility codes are matched to canonical facility IDs via detloc_lookup_full. Match coverage for the 620 shared codes is the same as documented for the feather file. The 87 parquet-only codes are covered by the same lookup where they have known DETLOCs; 28 remain unclassified.
